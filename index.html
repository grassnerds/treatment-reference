Product Safety & Customer Talk Tracks
Know this cold. If a customer asks "is this stuff safe?" you should never say "I'm not sure."

The #1 Question: "Do you use Roundup / glyphosate?"

Answer: No. Grass Nerds does not use glyphosate (Roundup) in any lawn care treatment round. Our weed control products are selective herbicides — they target weeds without harming the turf. Glyphosate is a non-selective herbicide that kills everything it touches, which is why we don't use it on lawns.

Never say: "I'm not sure what we use" or "Everything is pet safe" without specifics. Customers who ask about glyphosate are informed — vague reassurance makes them trust you less, not more.

What We Actually Use — By Category
Learn the names so you can speak with confidence on calls.

🌱 Pre-Emergent Herbicides

These create a barrier in the soil to prevent weed seeds from germinating. They do NOT kill existing plants.

  • Prodiamine (Resolute 4FL) — Used in W1, C1, F2, fall granular rounds. Prevents crabgrass, goosegrass, and other annual weeds.
  • Dithiopyr (Dimension 2EW) — Used in W2, C2, F3. Also prevents crabgrass; has some early post-emergent activity.
  • 0-0-7 w/ Prodiamine granular — Fall pre-emergent + potassium. Prevents winter annuals.

⚠️ Post-Emergent Herbicides (Weed Killers)

These kill weeds that are already growing. All are selective — they kill weeds but NOT the turf.

  • Escalade 2 — 3-way broadleaf herbicide (W1, F1 spot, F7). Kills dandelions, clover, chickweed, etc.
  • Change Up — Broadleaf + sedge control (W3, F2-F4, C1-C2, C6).
  • Celsius — Warm-season safe post-emergent (W4-W6, C3-C5 spot treat). Excellent on tough weeds.
  • Cool Power — Cool-weather broadleaf control (W7).
  • Metsulfuron Methyl (MSM Turf) — Low-rate broadleaf control (W1, C1-C2).
  • Negate — Targets grassy weeds in warm season turf (W2).
  • Tribute Total — Broad-spectrum warm-season herbicide (W6 spot treat).
  • Sim-Trol 4L — Used when temps are below 75°F (W1, W7, C6).
  • Tenacity — Used in fescue for spot treatment (F6). Controls crabgrass + broadleaf.
  • Pro Sedge / Sedgehammer — Targets nutsedge specifically (W3, W5 spot treat).

🌿 Fertilizers

Nutrition for the turf. These do NOT contain any herbicides unless specifically combined in a round.

  • 25-0-12 w/ 85% poly — Slow-release N + K (W3).
  • 24-0-5 w/ 50% XCU — Controlled-release nitrogen + iron (W4).
  • 25-0-0 w/ Kelp — Liquid nitrogen + kelp extract (W5, F2-F3, F7, fungicide rounds).
  • 24-0-5 w/ Iron — Granular with iron for color (F1, F6, C3).
  • 8-14-4 w/ Lime — Phosphorus-heavy + lime for soil pH (F5).
  • Harrell's Stress Relief 0-0-25 — High potassium for stress tolerance (C6).
  • Earth Max — Soil health / biostimulant (F4, C4, shrub rounds).

💊 Fungicides

Preventative disease control. Applied on a schedule to stop fungus BEFORE it appears.

  • Propiconazole 41.8 — Systemic fungicide (WFP1, WFP3, PF1, PF5).
  • Xzemplar — Broad-spectrum fungicide (WFP2).
  • Azoxystrobin (Azoxy) — Strobilurin fungicide (PF2-PF4).
  • Activator Plus — Surfactant/adjuvant paired with fungicides.
  • Enclave — Multi-site contact fungicide used in shrub care (SC1-SC5).

🐛 Insecticides

  • Bifenthrin — Used for grubs, armyworms, perimeter pest, flea/tick.
  • Triple Crown / Merit — Systemic insecticide in shrub care (SC1, SC4).
  • Altus — Low-impact insecticide for shrub pests (SC2-SC3).

💬 Adjuvants & Surfactants

These help products stick and absorb. They're what might feel "sticky" on bare feet if walked on too soon.

  • Non-Ionic Surfactant — Helps spray adhere to leaf surfaces. Used in nearly every liquid round.
  • Sticker — Similar purpose, used in some fescue rounds.

Re-Entry & Safety Talk Track

When a customer asks "Is it safe for my kids / pets?", here's what to say:

  • "All of our products are labeled safe for residential use, including around children and pets."
  • "We recommend staying off treated areas until the product dries — usually about 30 minutes to an hour depending on conditions."
  • "The surfactant we use can feel a little sticky underfoot if you walk on it before it dries, but once it's dry there's no issue."
  • "We do not use glyphosate or Roundup. Our weed control products are selective herbicides — they target weeds specifically and don't harm the grass or pose the concerns associated with glyphosate."
  • "If you'd like, I can send you a list of the specific products we use on your lawn so you can review them."

Do NOT say: "It's all pet safe / kid safe" as a blanket statement without specifics. Informed customers hear that as a dodge. Name the product category (selective herbicide, pre-emergent, etc.) and the dry-time guideline. Specificity = trust.

Add-On Services
Additional services beyond the core treatment programs

🧬 Soil Sample

When: Anytime, once annually

What: Samples from 4–6 spots, sent to lab. Tells us what nutrients the soil has, what's missing, and pH balance.

Customer value: "This lets us build a custom plan instead of guessing. Better soil = thicker grass, better color, fewer problems."

🐛 Grub & Armyworm Prevention

When: May–October (1 application)

Type: Liquid insecticide

What: Targets grubs and armyworms at the larva stage before they hatch and feed in fall.

🌱 Core Aeration (Warm Season)

When: June–August

What: 2–4" plugs pulled to loosen compacted soil and circulate nutrients deeper. Leave plugs on lawn.

Customer value: "This lets water, air, and nutrients reach the roots. Stronger roots = thicker, healthier turf."

🌱 Core Aeration + Overseeding (Fescue)

When: September–October

What: Aerate + plant new tall fescue seed. Fills thin areas and improves thickness.

Customer value: "Fall is the ideal window for fescue seed. The aeration gives it perfect seed-to-soil contact."

🦟 Mosquito Control

When: April–October, every 28–35 days (7 applications)

Type: Fog application

Customer value: "Makes your outdoor space more enjoyable. Targets mosquitoes as they hatch."

🔥 Fire Ant Prevention

When: Anytime, typically March–June (1 application)

Type: Granular insecticide

What: Eliminates colonies at the source. Protection up to 12 months. Works gradually — targets the queen.

🐾 Flea & Tick Prevention

When: Anytime, approximately quarterly (4 applications)

Type: Liquid barrier treatment

What: Reduces fleas and ticks in grass and shaded areas. Contact kill + residual prevention.

🏠 Perimeter Pest Prevention

When: Anytime, approximately quarterly (4 applications)

Type: Liquid barrier around foundation

What: Stops crawling pests from entering the home. Applied around the foundation perimeter.

🌻 Garden Bed Weed Prevention

When: Round 1: March–April · Round 2: September–October

Type: Granular pre-emergent

What: Creates a barrier in garden beds to prevent weed seeds from sprouting.

✅ Growth Regulator (Bermuda & Zoysia)

When: June, July, August (3 applications)

Type: Liquid

What: Slows top growth, strengthens roots, more even growth, denser turf, better color. Reduces mowing frequency.

🌾 Fescue Overseeding

When: September–October

What: Fescue seed at 5 lbs / 1,000 sq ft. Thickens thin areas and replenishes the lawn with new grass.
